Why it matters

One medical emergency can change everything

A single hospital stay for a heart surgery or cancer treatment can cost Rs. 5–15 lakh or more in India. Without health insurance, most families would need to drain their savings, sell assets, or borrow at high interest rates to cover these expenses.

Health insurance is not just about paying premiums — it is about protecting your family's financial future. With the right cover, you get access to quality treatment at network hospitals without worrying about the bill. You also benefit from preventive checkups, no-claim bonuses, and tax savings under Section 80D.

Government schemes like Ayushman Bharat already cover over 50 crore Indians. But millions more remain uninsured simply because they do not know their options.

Glossary

Key health insurance terms at a glance

Understanding these basic terms will help you compare plans with confidence and avoid surprises when you need to make a claim.

Sum Insured

The maximum amount your insurer will pay for medical expenses in a policy year. Choose a sum insured that reflects current hospital costs in your city.

Premium

The annual or monthly amount you pay to keep your health insurance policy active. Premiums vary by age, coverage amount, add-ons, and medical history.

Waiting Period

A predetermined period after policy purchase during which certain conditions are not covered. Typically 30 days for general illnesses, 2–4 years for pre-existing diseases.

Copay

A cost-sharing arrangement where you pay a fixed percentage of the claim amount and the insurer pays the rest. Plans with copay usually have lower premiums.

Network Hospital

A hospital that has a tie-up with your insurer to provide cashless treatment. You can walk in, show your health card, and get treated without paying upfront.

No-Claim Bonus

A reward for every claim-free year. Your sum insured increases by a fixed percentage (usually 50%) without any additional premium, up to a maximum limit.

Cashless Claim

The insurer settles the hospital bill directly with the network hospital. You only pay for non-covered items. This is the most convenient type of claim.

Reimbursement

You pay the hospital bill yourself and submit the documents to your insurer for repayment. Usually takes 7–15 days after all documents are received.

FAQ

Common questions answered

Is health insurance mandatory in India?

Not mandatory for all citizens, but highly recommended. Some categories have mandatory coverage (e.g., ESI for eligible employees). The government provides free coverage under Ayushman Bharat for qualifying families. Without insurance, a single hospitalisation can wipe out years of savings.

Can I claim tax benefits on health insurance premiums?

Yes, under Section 80D. Up to Rs. 25,000 for self, spouse, and children (Rs. 50,000 if senior citizen). Additional Rs. 25,000 for parents (Rs. 50,000 if senior). Maximum deduction: Rs. 1,00,000.

What is a waiting period in health insurance?

A waiting period is the time you must wait after buying a policy before certain covers apply. Standard: 30 days for general illnesses, 1–2 years for specified conditions, 2–4 years for pre-existing diseases.

What is the difference between cashless and reimbursement claims?

In a cashless claim, the insurer pays the hospital directly — you just show your health card at a network hospital and walk out without paying. In a reimbursement claim, you pay the bill yourself and later submit documents to the insurer for repayment. Cashless is more convenient, but only available at network hospitals. Reimbursement works at any hospital, but requires you to arrange funds upfront and wait 7–15 days for repayment.

Do I need health insurance if my employer already covers me?

Yes, for several reasons. Employer coverage usually ends when you leave or lose your job. It often has low sum insured limits (Rs. 3–5 lakh) that may not be enough for serious illnesses. It also does not cover your family members in most cases. Having an independent policy ensures continuous cover, builds no-claim bonus, and covers you between jobs. You can also claim tax benefits under Section 80D on your own policy.
